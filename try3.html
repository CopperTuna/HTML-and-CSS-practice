Clown Fish
Common clown fish, (Amphiprion ocellaris), speciesof anemone fish best known for its striking orange and white coloration and its mutualismwith certain species of sea anemones.
Brown Pencilfish
Their common names came from their slanted swimming style in which the head points toward the surface and the tail dips down at a 45-degree angle.
Silver Hatchetfish
Their body is shiny silver, narrow, and curved like the blade part of a hatchet. They tend to surf around the water surface with their fins out like little wings, looking for small foods floating up top.
Golden Wonder Killifish
Aplocheilus lineatus is a gorgeous (and hardy) centerpiece fish that gets up to 4 inches (10cm) long. The male is more colorful and has a brilliant yellow body with a blue-green sheen and orange edging on its tail and fins.
African Butterfly Fish
Growing up to 5 inches (13 cm) in length, the freshwater butterfly fish belongs in a 30-gallon or larger aquarium with no small tank mates.
Furcata Rainbowfish
They're very fast and eat almost anything, so don't mix them with slower fish like long-tailed guppies that might get outcompeted during mealtimes.
Betta Fish
Yes, bettas will technically swim all over the aquarium, but if your tank is set up correctly, they do prefer to hang out in the upper third level.
Common Danio
"Common" danios refer to zebra, leopard, blue, and other fast-paced danios with a narrow, torpedo-shaped body.
Clown Killifish
Like other killifish, it needs a close-fitting top to prevent escape and can live in cooler temperatures from 74-76°F (23-24°C).
Orange Hatchet Danio
Its shiny orange body has a distinct horizontal stripe running down the side made of several black spots in a row.
Halfbeak
Some halfbeak species require brackish water, so do your research and stick with the Celebes, silver, and golder halfbeaks for freshwater only tanks.
Guppy Fish
Guppies are live-bearing, so your children can get a biology lesson, too. Some varieties have elaborate fins and brilliant colors to make them fun to watch.
Molly Fish
Like the Guppy, he is peaceful and forgiving of water conditions. He gets a bit larger, growing up to 2 inches if he has the real estate.
Swordtail Fish
The Swordtail is also a live-bearing fish. However, he's not aggressive, as his name may suggest. It refers to the long tail that the male has.
